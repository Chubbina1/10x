What is programming

Programming is the process of designing and building software solutions to solve problems or perform specific tasks. At its heart, programming is about logic, creativity, and precision. Developers write instructions, called code, using languages like Python, JavaScript, or C++. These instructions tell computers how to perform tasks, from displaying websites to running complex algorithms. Programming isn't just for creating software—it also fosters critical thinking and problem-solving skills. As technology advances, learning to program becomes an essential skill in almost every field, empowering individuals to innovate, automate, and bring their ideas to life. It's more than a skill—it's a superpower.

Did you know that Python is one of the most popular programming languages for beginners due to its simple syntax? The first-ever programmer, Ada Lovelace, wrote an algorithm in the 1840s for a mechanical computer.
